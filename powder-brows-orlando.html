What it is

Powder Brows at Brows By Clari

Powder brows — also called ombré or microshading — create a soft, filled-in look similar to brow powder or pencil. Using a machine, pigment is applied in tiny dots that build from a lighter front to a defined tail, giving a polished, gradient finish.

It's our most versatile technique: ideal for oily skin, mature skin and anyone who loves a fuller, defined brow that holds beautifully over time.

Good to Know

Powder Brows FAQ

What's the difference between powder brows and microblading?

Microblading creates crisp individual hairstrokes for a natural look; powder brows create a soft, shaded, filled-in 'makeup' look. Powder brows also tend to last longer and suit oily skin better.

How long do powder brows last?

Typically 2 to 4 years, depending on skin type and aftercare, with an occasional refresh to maintain depth of color.

Will they look too dark at first?

Brows appear bolder for the first several days, then soften by 30–40% as they heal into a natural, even tone.

Can I combine powder and hairstrokes?

Yes — a 'combo brow' blends hairstrokes at the front with shading through the body and tail. We'll recommend the best approach at your consultation.
